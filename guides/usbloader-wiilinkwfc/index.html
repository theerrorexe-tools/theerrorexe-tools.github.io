How to setup WiiLink WFC on USB Loaders.

Here you learn how to use WiiLink WFC on USB Loaders. In this Guide i am using USB Loader GX.

WARNING: I am not responsible for any damages on your wii. WARNING 2: I am not affiliated with WiiLink24. WiiLink24 official Site: wiilink24.com

Requirements

  • A SD Card or an USB Stick with your USB Loader. Make sure you can load Games with no problems
  • A SSD/HDD with your dumped Games (optional)
  • A modded Wii Wii Modding Guide
  • A Dumped Game that works with WiiLink WFC
  • A Computer (with Linuxüêß/macOS üíª/ or Windows ü™ü) That can access the SD Card or USB Stick

Steps

  • 1. Go to wfc.wiilink24.com and klick the Download Button.
  • 2. Click on Search Patchers
  • 3. Click on Search any Wii Title and search for your Wii Game. Make sure that you click on your Region
  • 4. Scroll down until you see Download patches. click on .gct on your Region.
  • 5. Go to your Computer, and create a folder named "codes" on the USB Stick/SD Card. Make sure the codes folder is not inside another folder. Put your downloaded .gct file inside the codes folder.
  • 6. Eject the SD Card/USB Stick and put it in your Wii.
  • 7. Open the Homebrew Channel on your Wii.
  • 8. Open USB Loader GX, and click on the settings icon at the bottom left.
  • 9. Go to Loader Settings and scroll down until you see Ocarina. Click on it. Make sure it says ON. Don't scroll too fast.
  • 10. Go back and click on the arrow to the right. Click on Custom Paths. Click on GCT Cheatcodes. If you are using a SD Card click on SD on the bottom, and select the codes folder and click OK. If you are using USB Stick click on USB on the bottom or on SD and then on USB, select codes and click OK.
  • Note: You need to do Step 9 and 10 every time you wan't to play on WiiLink WFC
  • 11. Go back two times and click on your game. Click Start
  • Note. If you are on Mario Kart Wii, and connecting for the first time, you will see a WiiLink WFC Info.

    • You are now connected to WiiLink WFC
